Chess is a two-player strategy board game that has been played for centuries, originating in India as "chaturanga" before evolving into the modern game. It involves a 64-square board with an 8x8 grid, and each player starts with 16 pieces: one king, one queen, two rooks, two knights, two bishops, and eight pawns. The objective of chess is to checkmate the opponent's king, meaning the king is under direct threat of capture and has no legal moves to escape. Each piece has unique movement patterns. The queen moves in any direction, the bishop diagonally, the knight in an "L" shape, and pawns only forward. The game requires deep planning, anticipation of the opponent's moves, and sharp tactical thinking. It has long been a symbol of intellectual prowess and has grown into a global competitive sport, with international tournaments and world championships drawing millions of fans. Chess also has a rich history of famous games, iconic players like Bobby Fischer and Garry Kasparov, and a variety of openings and strategies that players can employ. It has become more accessible than ever with online platforms, allowing people to play or learn the game anytime, anywhere.
